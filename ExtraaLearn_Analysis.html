Dominant Finding

Channel of First Contact
Defines the Outcome

Of all the variables analyzed, how a lead first discovered ExtraaLearn is the single strongest predictor of conversion. Website-first leads convert at 4.3× the rate of mobile app leads — a statistically overwhelming gap that should drive acquisition strategy.

Key finding

Website-first leads convert at 45.6% vs. 10.5% for mobile app leads (χ² = 668.1, p < 0.001, Cramér's V = 0.381). This is the largest effect size of any variable in the dataset — bigger than profile completeness, time on site, or occupation.

Recommendations

1 — Prioritize website acquisition over app

The 4.3× conversion lift for website-first leads is the single highest-ROI lever available. Rebalancing paid acquisition toward web channels should be the first budget decision.

2 — Score and tier every incoming lead

Deploy the XGBoost model to score leads in real time. Route top 40% to direct sales outreach; route 41–60% to automated nurture; deprioritize the bottom 40% entirely.

3 — Optimize for time-on-site, not volume

Time on website (d = 0.692) dwarfs all other engagement signals. Landing page quality and content depth matter more than the number of visits or page views per session.

4 — Audit media spend for zero-signal channels

Print media type 1, print type 2, digital media, and educational channels show statistically negligible conversion association. These budgets should be reviewed against direct response alternatives.
