Dominant Finding

Channel of First Contact
Defines the Outcome

Of all the variables analyzed, how a lead first discovered ExtraaLearn is the single strongest predictor of conversion. Website-first leads convert at 4.3× the rate of mobile app leads. This is a statistically overwhelming gap that should drive acquisition and resource prioritization strategy.

Key finding

Website-first leads convert at 45.6% vs. 10.5% for mobile app leads (χ² = 668.1, p < 0.001, Cramér's V = 0.381). This is the largest effect size of any variable in the dataset, bigger than profile completeness, time on site, or occupation.

Why XGBoost over Decision Tree despite lower raw recall

Decision Tree caught 372 converters (TP) vs. XGBoost's 364, but also generated 251 false positives vs. 182. XGBoost's FN rate of 49 (vs. DT's 41) is the trade-off for substantially better precision (66.7% vs. 59.7%) and a 3× more stable cross-validated recall (87.9% ±2.3% vs. 87.0% ±5.6%). In production, the precision gap translates directly to fewer wasted outreach calls.

Model Comparison

Four Models, One Winner

All four models were trained with 5-fold stratified cross-validation. Recall was the primary tuning metric throughout: missing a likely converter costs ~$5,000 in lost revenue vs. ~$50 for a wasted call. Logistic Regression regularization (C, penalty) was tuned via GridSearchCV for a fair comparison with the ensemble models. Decision Tree and Random Forest used GridSearchCV; XGBoost used RandomizedSearchCV with 50 candidates. Final metrics are from a held-out test set of 1,384 leads (30%). XGBoost was selected for its highest ROC-AUC (0.920) and best cross-validated recall (87.9% ± 2.3%). The Decision Tree had higher single-run test recall (90.1%) but nearly 3× more variance (±5.6%), indicating instability in production.

Decision Tree Logic

How the Pruned Decision Tree Splits Leads

The best-fit Decision Tree (max depth = 3, tuned via GridSearchCV) uses just 4 features to classify all 4,612 leads. Reading left to right across the leaves reveals the clearest conversion logic in the dataset: website channel and time on site dominate every branch.

Tree insight: website + high time + no phone activity = 90.9% conversion

The most surprising branch: website + low time + medium profile = 2.8%, nearly zero. A medium-profile lead who hasn't invested time is actually less likely to convert than the average 30% baseline, even though they came through the best channel. Profile completion signals intent; medium completion on the website suggests a browsing-only mindset. Compare this to the website + high time + no phone activity leaf at 90.9%: a 32× difference driven by two splits.

Recommendations

1. Prioritize website acquisition over app

The 4.3× conversion lift for website-first leads is the single highest-ROI lever available. Rebalancing paid acquisition toward web channels should be the first budget decision.

2. Score and tier every incoming lead

Deploy the XGBoost model to score leads in real time. Route top 40% to direct sales outreach; route 41–60% to automated nurture; deprioritize the bottom 40% entirely.

3. Optimize for time-on-site, not volume

Time on website (d = 0.692) dwarfs all other engagement signals. Landing page quality and content depth matter more than the number of visits or page views per session.

4. Audit media spend for zero-signal channels

Print media type 1, print type 2, digital media, and educational channels show statistically negligible conversion association. These budgets should be reviewed against direct response alternatives.
